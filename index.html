Earth's Pollinators, Insects & Beneficial Friends of the Pacific Northwest

By KimmySomeLove42

This engaging coloring book is perfect for young nature enthusiasts and budding artists alike. Dive into the fascinating lives of bees, butterflies, beetles, and other incredible insects that play a vital role in our ecosystem.

Discover fun facts about these amazing pollinators of the Pacific Northwest and learn how they help plants grow and thrive. For example, did you know that one bee can visit 5,000 flowers in a single day?

This coloring book is not only a fun activity but also an educational journey into the world of beneficial insects. Each page is designed to inspire creativity, while teaching kids about the importance of pollinators in our natural world.

About the Book

Fascinating Pollinator Facts

  • Bees can recognize human faces!
  • A single bee can visit up to 5,000 flowers in one day.
  • Butterflies taste with their feet.
  • Pollinators are responsible for 1 out of 3 bites of food we eat.

Filled with fun riddles, facts, and beautiful illustrations, this book blends learning with creativity. Each insect and pollinator is introduced with friendly, whimsical art that children can color, while also learning about their real-life roles in nature.

It’s the perfect blend of science and art, designed for ages 4–12, but enjoyable for the whole family!
